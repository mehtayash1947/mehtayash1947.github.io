I have been fortunate enough to have been persuaded by my parents to learn Hindustani Classical vocal. I have been learning Hindustani Classical Vocal since the 2nd grade, which (without any ego, but simply stating the facts) makes my 12+ years (and counting) experience in the field.

Many people approach this field in a casual way and skim only the surface, but, as one the all time legends of Classical Music Pt. Rajan Mishra's father (another legend) Pt. Hanuman Prasad Mishra used to say, "Beta, abhi sur ki chont lagi nahi hai, fir dekho apne aap riyaz karne lagoge". Very few people invest a good amount of time into Indian Classical Music, but even among them, a very scarce number of people are blessed to have been hit with "sur ki chont". I am very fortunate to have experienced it.

I also have an avid interest and play a decent standard of Tabla, and listen to other Hindustani Classical instruments, especially Flute, Santoor, Sitar and Sarod.

Here, I have tried to pen down some of my thoughts and insights related to Music, many times specific to Hindustani Classical. These things I have very carefully written from my deep experience and are very esoteric so I expect you to do your part too by reading them with all your attention, carefully and with an open mind. I do not expect more than a very small percentage of people to really understand them. Nevertheless, they are here for anyone's knowledge; please feel free to reach out to me regarding any concerns/criticism/doubts regarding the same.
